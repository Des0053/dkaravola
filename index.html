  • About Me

    My name is Despina Karavola and I currently am a PhD student in the physics department of the University of Athens, Greece.
    My main insterest that, also, constitutes the main topic of my dissertation is High Energy Astrophysics.
    My research is focused on photo-hadronic interactions in relativistic magnetic reconnection in AGN.
    I'm particularly interested in designing numerical simulations and/or numerically solving complicated systems of equations for that purpose.

  • CV

    I was born and raised in Athens, Greece.

    I acquired my Bachelor degree in 2021, from the Nationaland Kapodistrian University of Athens, majoring in physics. Aspiring of persuing astrophysics, my BSc thesis was focused on particle acceleration of relativistic particles inside magnetic islands that are created in higlhy magnetized astrophysical environments.

    In 2021, I enrolled in MSc astrophysics program the University of Athens from which I graduated in 2023. During these years I worked with assistant professor Maria Petropoulou who supervised my thesis. The latter has as it main interest the role of photo-hadronic interactions in Active Galatic Nuclei.

    Currently, I am doing my PhD under the supervision of M. Petropoulou, L.Sironi and D. Giannios in the University of Athens. The main topic of my dissertation is related to hadronic acceleration properties, simulated by Particle-In-Cell simulations, as well as, modeling of AGN environments includind highly relativistic hadron poplations.
